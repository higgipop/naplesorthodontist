Why Adults Are Choosing Orthodontics Now

The American Association of Orthodontists estimates that adults now represent over 27% of orthodontic patients — the highest proportion ever recorded. Drivers include better treatment options (Invisalign and clear aligners removed the social stigma), more flexible financing, and growing awareness that crowded or misaligned teeth affect long-term oral health, not just appearance.

Adults in Naples are pursuing orthodontic treatment for a range of reasons: closing gaps, correcting bite issues that cause jaw pain, preparing for restorative work like implants or veneers, or simply wanting a confidence boost in professional and social settings.

Adult treatment works. Teeth move at any age. Bone density in adults is higher than in teens, which can slightly extend treatment timelines — but outcomes are fully comparable.

What's Different About Adult Treatment

Adult orthodontic cases differ from teen cases in a few important ways:

  • No growth to leverage. Jaw growth in children can assist tooth movement; adult treatment works with a fully developed skeleton.
  • Existing dental work. Crowns, veneers, implants, and missing teeth all affect treatment planning. Your orthodontist needs to work around or coordinate with existing restorations.
  • Gum health matters more. Adults with gum disease or bone loss need clearance from a periodontist before starting orthodontic treatment.
  • Compliance is generally better. Adults consistently outperform teens on Invisalign wear-time compliance, which offsets slightly slower movement.

Cost & Insurance for Adults in Naples

Most orthodontic insurance policies cover adult treatment at the same rate as children — typically 25–50% up to a lifetime maximum (usually $1,500–$3,000). Some older employer plans do exclude adults; verify your benefits before your consultation.

FSA and HSA funds can be applied to orthodontic treatment costs, and most Naples orthodontists offer 0% in-house financing for 12–24 months. CareCredit is also widely accepted. See our full cost guide for payment plan details.

Adult Orthodontics FAQs
Common questions from Naples adult patients
Is it too late to get braces as an adult?
No. Teeth move at any age. Adults now represent more than 1 in 4 orthodontic patients in the U.S. Adult bone density can mean slightly longer treatment times, but outcomes are fully comparable to teen treatment.
What is the most discreet orthodontic option for adults?
Invisalign and clear aligners are nearly invisible in normal conversation. Ceramic (tooth-colored) braces are less visible than metal. Lingual braces (bonded to the back of teeth) are completely hidden from the front but are less widely available and cost more.
Does dental insurance cover orthodontics for adults?
Most plans do — typically 25–50% up to a lifetime maximum. Some older plans exclude adults. FSA and HSA funds always apply. Verify your specific plan at the free consultation.
How long does adult orthodontic treatment take?
Most adult cases take 18–24 months. Minor corrections can finish in 6–12 months. Compliance with Invisalign wear time (20–22 hrs/day) is the biggest variable.
Will I need a retainer forever?
Yes — permanent retainer wear is standard practice. Most orthodontists prescribe nightly wear after an initial period of full-time wear. A fixed bonded retainer on the lower front teeth is common and maintenance-free.
Can I get Invisalign if I have crowns or veneers?
Often yes, but existing dental work affects planning. Crowns and veneers require different attachment bonding considerations, and certain movements may be limited. Your orthodontist will assess at the consultation.
