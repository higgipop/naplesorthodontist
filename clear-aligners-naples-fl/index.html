Clear Aligners vs. Invisalign — What's the Difference?

Invisalign is a brand. Clear aligners is the category. Think of it like "Kleenex" vs. "facial tissue" — Invisalign pioneered the technology and remains the most widely used system, but several competing brands now offer similar treatment using comparable aligner materials.

All clear aligner systems work the same way: a series of custom-fitted plastic trays apply gentle pressure to gradually move teeth into the correct position. You swap to the next tray every 1–2 weeks, visiting your orthodontist every 6–8 weeks to monitor progress.

Are You a Good Candidate?

Clear aligners work well for most adults and teens. They're particularly well-suited for:

  • Mild to moderate crowding or spacing
  • Mild overbite, underbite, or crossbite
  • Patients who want a discreet, removable option
  • Adults with busy professional or social schedules
  • Teens with strong compliance (worn 20–22 hrs/day)

Clear aligners may not be the right choice for:

  • Severe skeletal discrepancies or jaw misalignment
  • Significant vertical tooth movement requirements
  • Young children (typically under 12) whose jaws are still developing
  • Patients unlikely to maintain required wear time

Clear Aligner FAQs
Common questions from Naples patients
What is the difference between clear aligners and Invisalign?
Invisalign is a brand of clear aligners — the most widely recognized one. The category includes brands like ClearCorrect, Spark, and 3M Clarity. All use removable plastic trays to shift teeth. Invisalign has the largest provider network and most clinical history; alternatives often cost less.
How much do clear aligners cost in Naples, FL?
Typical range is $3,000–$8,000 depending on brand and case complexity. Invisalign runs $4,000–$8,000. Alternative brands can be $500–$1,500 less. Most practices offer 0% financing for 12–24 months.
Are clear aligners as effective as braces?
For mild to moderate alignment issues, yes. Complex bite corrections or severe crowding may still require traditional braces. Your Naples orthodontist will evaluate your case at the free consultation and recommend the best option.
How long does clear aligner treatment take?
Most cases take 12–18 months. Minor corrections can finish in 6 months. Compliance is the biggest variable — wearing aligners fewer than 20–22 hours per day extends treatment significantly.
Can I eat and drink normally with clear aligners?
Yes — aligners are removed for all eating and drinking except water. There are no food restrictions. Brush and rinse before reinserting each time to prevent staining and tooth decay.
