What Is Invisalign?

Invisalign is a system of custom-made, clear plastic aligners that gradually shift your teeth into position. Unlike traditional braces, there are no wires or brackets — the aligners are nearly invisible and can be removed for eating, brushing, and flossing.

Naples orthodontists who offer Invisalign are certified by Align Technology and trained to handle everything from simple spacing issues to more complex bite corrections. Provider tier (Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond) reflects their volume of Invisalign cases — higher-tier providers have more hands-on experience.

Naples tip: Ask your provider what Invisalign tier they are. Diamond and Platinum providers in Collier County have treated hundreds of cases and can handle more complex corrections.

Is Invisalign Right for You?

Invisalign works well for:

  • Mild to moderate crowding and spacing
  • Adults and teens who want a discreet option
  • Patients with good compliance habits (aligners must be worn 20–22 hrs/day)
  • Minor bite issues (overbite, underbite, crossbite)

Traditional braces may be recommended for severe crowding, complex bite corrections, or younger children who aren't ready for the responsibility of removable aligners. Your Naples orthodontist will advise after a free consultation.

All Invisalign providers are certified by Align Technology, but experience varies significantly. When comparing providers in Naples and Collier County, ask about:

  • Number of Invisalign cases completed
  • Before-and-after photo examples for similar cases
  • Whether they use the latest iTero scanner for digital impressions (no goop)
  • Retainer plan after treatment is complete
Invisalign FAQs for Naples Patients
Common questions before your first appointment
How much does Invisalign cost in Naples, FL?
Invisalign in Naples typically costs $4,000–$8,000 depending on case complexity and treatment length. Many orthodontists offer payment plans starting at $100–$200/month with no interest.
Is Invisalign as effective as braces?
For most mild to moderate alignment issues, Invisalign is equally effective as traditional braces. Complex bite corrections may still require braces. Your Naples orthodontist will advise after a free consultation and exam.
How long does Invisalign treatment take?
Most Invisalign treatments take 12–18 months. Minor corrections (Invisalign Lite) can be completed in as little as 6 months. Teen and comprehensive cases may take up to 24 months.
Does insurance cover Invisalign in Florida?
Most Florida orthodontic insurance plans cover Invisalign the same as traditional braces — typically 25–50% of costs up to the plan's lifetime orthodontic maximum. We'll help you verify your coverage during the free consult.
